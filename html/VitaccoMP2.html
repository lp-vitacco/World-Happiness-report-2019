The 2019 World Happiness Report

Finland

The information discussed on this page can be found on the World Happiness Report Website. The data was downloaded from Kaggle.

The World Happiness Report is a collection of data from Gallup World Poll published by the Sustainable Development Solutions Network. The data collected is used to analyze the happiness levels of nations across the world over the years. This study focuses on the 2019 World Happiness Report with a focus on Finland, which was rated the number one happiest country of the year.

The data that will be analyzed is based off on a survey conducted by the Gallup World Poll group. The survey was structured using the Cantril life ladder method. The participants were asked to think of the responses like a ladder, with 10 being the top or best and 0 being the bottom or worst. With this survey and other background information, the researchers created a countries overall Happiness Score. After determining this they conducted further investigation on what factors might impact the scores the most. The factors they judged each country on was: GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and perceptions of corruption. They determined what was the level of influence was for each factor in each country. The numbers I work with in the dataset reflect how the researchers thought that factor influenced the overall happiness score.

I decided to focus on Finland because I am interested to see how the six factors influenced the highest ranking country. I want to if other top countries are influenced similarly.
